The Uncanny Valley.

The framework I propose — not for people, for tasks

Three levels of delegation.

Criterion In this task, is the human the end — or the means? — after Kant, Groundwork, 1785

§ 01

Automate

The human is a pure tool here.

Dangerous, toxic, repetitive tasks. Automating them returns dignity to the worker.

§ 02

Augment

The task needs human judgment.

Robot provides physical capability. The decision stays with the person.

§ 03

Reserve

The human is the meaning here.

Care, art, moral weight, relationships. Delegating is not efficiency — it is loss.

LEVEL § 01

Automate

Where the human is pure tool — the robot returns dignity.

  • MINING — coal, oil, uranium · ~300 deaths/yr in Chinese mines alone
  • NUCLEAR CLEANUP — Fukushima, Chernobyl decommissioning
  • DEMINING + RECON — military and post-conflict
  • HEAVY ASSEMBLY — Foxconn (14 suicides · 2010) · warehousing
  • HAZARDOUS AGRO — pesticide spraying, toxic processing
  • DEEP-SEA · SPACE — environments human bodies cannot survive
"A living tool is not a life."
— Aristotle, Politics I.4 · ὄργανον ἔμψυχον
LEVEL § 02

Augment

Extend the human — do not erase them.

Example — one task, three levels: surgery
  1. Mechanical cut — automate
  2. Precision under supervision — augment
  3. Decision to stop the operation — human, always
  • SURGERY — da Vinci system · 15M+ procedures to date
  • CONSTRUCTION — robotic arms + human foreman
  • INDUSTRIAL INSPECTION — where G1 earns its keep in KZ
  • LONG-HAUL FREIGHT — highway autonomy + urban human
  • TIER-1 SUPPORT — machine filters, human decides

G1 doing industrial inspection
"Technology is a way of revealing. The hand extends."
— Martin Heidegger, The Question Concerning Technology · 1954
LEVEL § 03

Reserve

Where the human is the meaning — not the executor.

  • EARLY CHILDHOOD — who is the child bonding with?
  • PSYCHOTHERAPY — simulated empathy is not empathy
  • ART — suffering is the precondition of expression
  • MORAL JUDGMENT — accountability cannot be delegated to a processor
  • CLERGY · FAMILY · LEADERSHIP — bonds, not tasks
"Action, not labor, is where humanity lives."
— Hannah Arendt, The Human Condition · 1958

Humanoid caregiver. Comfort built on simulated empathy.

Honest machine utility. No deception · no attachment · no comfort.

Engineering honesty — before the position

Limits of my view.

Three places where my framework stops working.

§ 01

Economics

Automation in Level 01 displaces workers. My framework does not answer what society owes them. Engineering alone cannot fix this.

§ 02

Culture

The uncanny valley is weaker in Japan than in the West. There is no universal curve. My argument is culturally situated, not universal law.

§ 03

Time

In twenty years we may trust robots more than people. I am describing 2026 — not 2046. The framework dates itself by design.

My position — after six months in the field · much may still change

Replace the hand.
Keep the face.
Replace the arm.
Keep the smile.

Humanoid form belongs in work — functionally, not emotionally.
The uncanny valley is not a wall to break through.
It is a line that protects us.

An engineering constraint, not a boast — I consciously choose not to imitate humans where it leads to cognitive deception.
